### 1.1 Account Strategy

#### @energyvampireblasters (Primary Growth Account)

**Role:** Discovery engine. This is where cold audiences find EVB through search, Reels, and Explore. The name itself is a keyword -- "energy vampire" is a search term that @iamdavidstrauss will never rank for.

**What goes here:**
- Educational Reels (30-sec hook questions, vampire type breakdowns)
- Carousels (tactical how-to content, "5 Signs" style posts)
- Quote graphics using brand trade dress (Hot Pink on Black, Cyan accents)
- Polls, quizzes, and interactive Stories
- All ManyChat-triggered content (reply VAMPIRE, reply ESCAPE)
- Product launches, challenge promotions, Detection Kit distribution

**Tone:** Empowering, direct, practical. Speaks TO the ICP's pain. Bold but compassionate.

**Funnel role:** Top of funnel. Attract -> educate -> convert to email list or DM automation.

**Posting cadence:** 5-7x/week (3 Reels, 2 carousels, 1-2 quote/graphic posts). Daily Stories.

#### @iamdavidstrauss (Authority / Personal Brand Account)

**Role:** Trust and credibility builder. This is where warm followers deepen their relationship with David the person. Behind-the-scenes, personal stories, author journey, philosophical takes.

**What goes here:**
- Personal stories about David's own energy vampire experiences
- Behind-the-scenes of writing, ghostwriting, the author life
- Deeper philosophical content on personal development
- Collaborations and features (podcast clips, speaking events)
- Occasional cross-posts that reference @energyvampireblasters
- Book Your Brand content (separate audience, separate business)

**Tone:** Authentic, vulnerable, aspirational. The person behind the brand.

**Funnel role:** Mid-funnel. Builds the trust that converts followers into paying customers.

**Posting cadence:** 3-4x/week. Not the growth priority right now.

#### Cross-Promotion Tactics

1. **Bio cross-linking.** @iamdavidstrauss bio includes "Creator of @energyvampireblasters." @energyvampireblasters bio includes "By bestselling author @iamdavidstrauss."
2. **Instagram Collab feature.** Use 1-2x/month for high-impact posts. The post appears on both feeds with shared engagement.
3. **Story breadcrumbs.** On @iamdavidstrauss, post Stories like: "Just dropped a deep dive on the 5 types of energy vampires over at @energyvampireblasters -- go watch." On @energyvampireblasters, humanize: "My founder @iamdavidstrauss shares the story behind why he wrote this book."
4. **Stagger posting times.** Never post on both accounts within 2 hours. Space them out so followers of both don't get overwhelmed.
5. **Different content, same message.** Same topic, different execution. @energyvampireblasters posts a carousel on the 5 vampire types. @iamdavidstrauss posts a personal story Reel about the time his best friend was an energy vampire. Same message, different angle.

#### Growth Priority

Lead with @energyvampireblasters. It is more discoverable for cold audiences than a personal name. Once it hits 5-10K followers, the cross-promotion to @iamdavidstrauss becomes a flywheel. Until then, David's creative energy goes to the niche account.

### 1.2 Content Pillars

Every piece of EVB content falls under one of these five pillars. They map directly to the customer journey: Awareness -> Recognition -> Action -> Transformation -> Community.

#### Pillar 1: RECOGNITION ("Do I have an energy vampire?")

Content that helps the ICP see their situation clearly for the first time. Pattern identification, vampire type breakdowns, red flag education.

- "5 Signs You're Dealing With a [Vampire Type]"
- Quiz-style content: "Which Energy Vampire Is Draining You?"
- Relatable scenarios the ICP has lived through
- The 5 Vampire Types: Emotional Manipulator, Guilt Tripper, Constant Critic, Drama Creator, Silent Controller

**Emotional hook:** "Oh my god, that's exactly what's happening to me."

#### Pillar 2: PROTECTION ("How do I stop the drain?")

Tactical, actionable boundary-setting content. Scripts, frameworks, exercises. This is where EVB differentiates from general "toxic relationship" content -- specific tools, not vague advice.

- Boundary scripts ("Say this when they guilt-trip you")
- The Walls & Bridges framework
- Energy shield techniques
- "What to do when..." scenario-based advice
- Daily energy protection habits

**Emotional hook:** "I can actually do something about this."

#### Pillar 3: INNER WORK ("What if the vampire is me?")

The most differentiated pillar. Most energy vampire content only talks about other people. David goes deeper: inner negative dialogue, self-sabotage patterns, addictions and habits that drain you from the inside.

- Inner vampire awareness (the chatter in your head)
- Self-sabotage pattern recognition
- Habits and addictions as energy vampires
- The beliefs that keep you stuck
- "You already know the answer" empowerment content

**Emotional hook:** "I never thought about it that way. What if I'm part of the problem?"

#### Pillar 4: TRANSFORMATION ("What does life look like after?")

Before/after stories, wins, breakthroughs. The "light at the end of the tunnel" content that gives the ICP hope and motivation to take action.

- Transformation stories (David's own + future community members)
- "After I blasted my energy vampire, this happened..."
- The 7-Day Challenge framework (tease the experience)
- Level-up moments and celebrations
- Vision of the "energy-rich" life

**Emotional hook:** "That could be me. I want that."

#### Pillar 5: COMMUNITY ("You're not alone in this.")

Content that builds tribe identity. Shared language, inside jokes, community moments, user-generated content. This pillar feeds the Skool community.

- "Tag someone who needs to hear this"
- Community wins and shoutouts
- David's coaching moments (Skool previews)
- Live Q&A recaps
- "Your tribe" vs "your vampires" contrast content

**Emotional hook:** "There are other people going through this. I belong here."

## How to Use This Document

1. **Before Day 1:** Set up ManyChat with VAMPIRE and ESCAPE keywords. Set up Kit for email capture. Have the Detection Kit PDF ready for delivery. Optimize both Instagram bios per Section 1.5.

2. **Each morning:** Open this document, find today's day number, read the row. You know the post type, the hook, what to say, and the CTA.

3. **For Reels:** Stand in front of your phone camera. Ask the hook question while looking at the lens. Deliver 20 seconds of value. End with the CTA. One take is fine. Authenticity beats production quality.

4. **For Carousels:** Use Canva with EVB brand colors (Hot Pink #FF1493, Cyan #00C8E8, Yellow #FFD700, Black #1A1A1A, Warm White #FAF5EF). Montserrat Bold for headlines, Source Sans 3 for body. Keep slides to 5-8 max.

5. **For Stories:** Spend 5-10 minutes daily. One poll or question sticker, one reshare of the day's post, one behind-the-scenes or personal moment.

6. **Batch recording:** Record all 3 weekly Reels in one sitting (same outfit, same location, 15 minutes total). Schedule via Meta Business Suite.

7. **Hashtags:** Copy-paste from the hashtag sets in Section 1.4. Rotate between Set A, B, and C.
