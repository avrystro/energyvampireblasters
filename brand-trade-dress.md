## 1. Brand Overview

### Brand Name
**Energy Vampire Blasters** (EVB)

### Tagline Options

1. **"Reclaim Your Energy. Reclaim Your Life."** -- Direct, action-oriented, speaks to the transformation. Recommended.
2. **"Stop the Drain. Start the Change."** -- Punchy, rhythmic, works well on social and merch.
3. **"Your Energy Is Sacred. Protect It."** -- Emotional, identity-level, resonates with the ICP's awakening moment.

### Brand Mission
Energy Vampire Blasters teaches people to identify and remove toxic relationships, addictions, negative habits, and destructive inner dialogue so they can reclaim their energy and build a life that feeds them instead of drains them.

### Brand Personality
- **Bold but compassionate.** We tell the truth directly, and we hold space while doing it.
- **Empowering, never victimizing.** The ICP is the hero. We hand them the weapons.
- **Metaphor-rich and vivid.** Vampires, energy shields, blasters, levels, boss fights. The gamified language makes hard topics approachable.
- **Warm authority.** David is a guide who has walked through his own darkness. He speaks from experience, not theory.

### Brand Archetypes
- **The Liberator** (primary) -- freeing people from toxic patterns
- **The Sage** -- teaching awareness and pattern recognition
- **The Warrior** -- fighting back, taking action, setting boundaries

### Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
Women 30+, at a turning point. Leaving or recovering from a toxic relationship. Realizing the patterns they've been repeating. Ready to change who they surround themselves with. Feeling stuck but motivated enough to seek help. They want someone who gets it, not clinical advice.

## 2. Visual Identity

### Logo

**Primary logo:** "Energy Vampire BLASTERS" wordmark. Clean text treatment, no monster eyes or horror elements. The word "BLASTERS" is emphasized (larger, bolder, or different color) to anchor the empowerment side of the brand.

**Logo Clear Space:** Maintain padding equal to the height of the letter "B" in BLASTERS on all four sides. No other text, graphics, or visual elements may enter this zone.

**Minimum Size:**
- Digital: 120px wide minimum
- Print: 1.5 inches wide minimum

**Logo Don'ts:**
- Do NOT stretch, skew, or distort the proportions
- Do NOT change individual letter colors outside the approved palette
- Do NOT add drop shadows, bevels, glows, or 3D effects
- Do NOT place on busy photographic backgrounds without a solid or semi-transparent backing
- Do NOT rotate the logo
- Do NOT recreate the logo in unapproved fonts
- Do NOT add taglines or other text inside the clear space zone

#### Color Usage Rules

**Headings:** EVB Hot Pink (#FF1493) on light backgrounds. White (#FFFFFF) on dark backgrounds.
**Body text:** EVB Black (#1A1A1A) or Soft Charcoal (#3D3D3D) on light backgrounds. Warm White (#FAF5EF) on dark backgrounds.
**CTA buttons:** EVB Hot Pink (#FF1493) background with white text. Hover state: darken 10%.
**Secondary buttons:** EVB Cyan (#00C8E8) background with EVB Black text.
**Backgrounds:** Warm White (#FAF5EF) for content areas. EVB Black (#1A1A1A) for hero sections, footers, and contrast blocks.
**Accents and highlights:** EVB Yellow (#FFD700) for badges, star ratings, "new" tags, and energy-positive callouts.
**Pull quotes and testimonials:** EVB Cyan (#00C8E8) left border, Soft Charcoal text.
**Cards and callout boxes:** Light Lavender (#E8D5F5) background with EVB Black text.

**Ratio guidance:**
- 60% neutrals (Warm White + Black + Charcoal)
- 25% brand primaries (Hot Pink + Cyan)
- 15% accents (Yellow + Lavender)

### Typography

#### Heading Font: **Montserrat** (Google Fonts, free)
- Weights: Bold (700) for H1/H2, SemiBold (600) for H3/H4
- Why: Geometric, modern, confident. Has the bold punch EVB needs without being aggressive. Excellent readability at large sizes. Works across web, social, and print.

#### Body Font: **Source Sans 3** (Google Fonts, free)
- Weights: Regular (400) for body, SemiBold (600) for emphasis
- Why: Designed for readability on screens. Humanist warmth that pairs well with Montserrat's geometry. Professional without being cold.

#### Type Scale

| Element | Font | Weight | Size (web) | Size (mobile) |

| H1 | Montserrat | Bold 700 | 48px | 32px |
| H2 | Montserrat | Bold 700 | 36px | 26px |

| Caption | Source Sans 3 | Regular 400 | 14px | 13px |

**Line height:** 1.6 for body text, 1.2 for headings.
**Letter spacing:** Normal for body. +0.5px for all-caps labels/overlines.

### Imagery Style

**What fits the brand:**
- Real women in moments of strength: standing tall, looking forward, arms open, taking up space
- Natural lighting, warm tones, golden hour
- Movement and action (walking away, breaking chains metaphorically, opening doors)
- Clean, uncluttered compositions with breathing room
- Lifestyle shots that feel candid, not posed
- Dark-to-light visual metaphors (tunnel to light, storm to calm, night to dawn)
- Shield/protection imagery done tastefully (not literal shields)

**What does NOT fit:**
- Gothic/horror vampire imagery (bats, fangs, blood, coffins)
- Dark, moody, heavily filtered photos
- Stock photos of people fake-smiling in business casual
- "Girl boss" aesthetic (rose gold everything, champagne, hustle culture)
- Clinical/therapy imagery (couches, clipboards, white coats)
- Overly soft/pastel/passive imagery
- Before/after weight-loss-style comparison photos

## 3. Voice & Tone

### How David Writes

David writes the way he talks: direct, warm, metaphor-driven, and deeply personal. He speaks TO the reader ("you"), not ABOUT them. He names the hard thing, then shows the way through it. He uses the vampire metaphor to make painful truths approachable.

**Voice pillars:**
1. **Direct.** No hedging, no "maybe consider possibly thinking about." Say the thing.
2. **Empathetic.** "I've been there" energy. He's walked through his own toxic patterns.
3. **No-BS.** Calls out what most people are too polite to say.
4. **Metaphor-rich.** Vampires, energy drains, shields, blasters, levels. The gamified language makes hard topics safe to explore.
5. **Empowering.** Every post, email, and chapter ends with the reader feeling stronger, not smaller.

### Words and Phrases to USE
- Energy vampire, energy drain, blaster, blast
- Reclaim, protect, shield, boundary
- Toxic, draining, suffocating
- Level up, boss fight, power-up, game over
- "You deserve better than this"
- "Here's what nobody tells you about..."
- "Let me be real with you"
- "You already know the answer"
- Escape, freedom, clarity, peace
- Inner vampire (the negative self-talk angle)
- Tribe, uplifters, your people

### Words and Phrases to AVOID
- Victim, survivor (too clinical, too passive)
- Narcissist (overused, triggers defensiveness in audience members who haven't accepted this yet)
- Healing journey (too soft/vague for the brand's edge)
- Manifest, vibration, universe (different lane)
- "Just leave" or any oversimplified advice
- Hustle, grind, boss babe
- Toxic masculinity / toxic femininity (too political)
- Any clinical psychology terms without context (gaslighting is OK because it's mainstream, but avoid DSM language)

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Instagram captions** | Bold, punchy, conversational. Hook in first line. End with a question or CTA. | "That person who leaves you feeling empty after every conversation? That's not a friendship. That's an energy vampire. Here's how to spot them before they drain you dry." |
| **Book content** | Warm authority, storytelling, deeper metaphor. Longer form, more personal anecdotes. | "I remember the exact moment I realized my best friend was my biggest energy vampire. We were sitting across from each other at dinner, and I felt more alone than if I'd been by myself." |
| **Email sequences** | Personal, one-to-one feel. Like a note from a friend who cares. Short paragraphs. | "Hey [name], I know what it feels like to wonder if you're the problem. You're not. But you ARE the solution. Let me show you what I mean." |
| **Coaching/Skool** | Supportive coach energy, direct feedback, celebratory when earned. | "You just identified your first energy vampire. That took guts. Now let's talk about what you're going to do about it." |
| **Sales pages** | Urgent but not pushy. Problem-agitate-solve. Paint the before/after vividly. | "You've read the articles. You've journaled about it. You know something has to change. This 7-day challenge is the moment you stop thinking about it and start doing it." |

## 4. Trade Dress Elements

Trade dress is the total visual impression that tells someone "this is Energy Vampire Blasters" before they read a single word. These elements, used together consistently, create protectable brand recognition.

### The EVB Signature Look

1. **Color combination:** Hot Pink + Cyan + Yellow on Black or Warm White. This specific 4-color combination on dark or cream backgrounds is the core trade dress element. No other personal development brand uses this palette.

2. **Typography pairing:** Montserrat Bold headlines + Source Sans 3 body. The contrast between geometric headings and humanist body text is part of the feel.

3. **Layout pattern:** Dark hero sections with Hot Pink headlines, transitioning to Warm White content sections with Black text. Cyan accents for interactive elements. Yellow for highlights and badges.

4. **Gamification language in design:** Level indicators, badge/shield graphics, progress markers. The "video game meets personal development" visual language is unique to EVB.

5. **Vampire archetype cards:** The 5 Energy Vampire types presented as character cards (like trading cards or character select screens). This format is ownable.

6. **Section dividers:** Cyan gradient line (left to right fade) between content sections.

### Building Trade Dress Protection

Start collecting evidence now:
- Save dated screenshots of every social post, web page, and email
- Keep copies of all PDFs, guides, and course materials
- Document when each design element was first used in commerce
- Screenshot any consumer comments referencing the visual brand ("I love the pink and blue" etc.)
- Keep receipts for all design work

## 5. Digital Application

### Instagram

**Grid aesthetic:** Alternate between three post types for a visually cohesive grid:
1. **Dark posts** -- EVB Black background, Hot Pink headline text, Cyan accent
2. **Light posts** -- Warm White background, EVB Black text, Yellow accent
3. **Photo posts** -- On-brand photography with a thin colored border or branded watermark

**Story templates:**
- Poll/question stories: Cyan background, white Montserrat text
- Quote stories: Black background, Hot Pink text, yellow highlight on key word
- Testimonial stories: Warm White background, Charcoal text, Cyan left stripe

**Reel covers:** Black background, Hot Pink headline in Montserrat Bold, centered. Consistent across all reels for recognizable grid.

**Highlight covers:** Simple icon (line-weight style) on solid color circles. One color per highlight category:
- Hot Pink: About / My Story
- Cyan: Tips / Education
- Yellow: Wins / Testimonials
- Lavender: Quiz / Free Stuff

### Website (energyvampireblasters.com) -- "Neon Warrior" Direction

**Design direction chosen (Session 66, Mar 2026):** Dark editorial with electric accents. Magazine-spread layouts on near-black backgrounds (#0a0a0a). Hot Pink + Cyan as neon accents. Yellow reserved for CTAs and highlight moments. Scroll progression moves dark-to-light (hero = in the drain, middle = awakening, bottom = liberation). Inspired by tonyrobbins.com (dark hero energy), onepeloton.com (neon accents on dark), danielle-laporte.com (editorial quality). The word "BLASTERS" demands kinetic energy -- this direction delivers it.

- **Hero section:** Deep black (#0a0a0a) with subtle radial color glows (pink at 6% opacity, cyan at 4%). Hook question as H1 in white, emphasis words in Hot Pink. Dual CTAs: primary (Detection Kit) in Hot Pink, secondary (Quiz) in outlined Cyan.
- **Content sections:** Alternate between #0a0a0a (deep black) and #1A1A1A (dark). No warm white sections -- the entire site is dark. Breathing room comes from spacing and opacity, not light backgrounds.
- **Navigation:** Sticky glassmorphism bar (rgba black at 92% + blur). White text at 60% opacity, Hot Pink on hover. Hot Pink CTA button. Mobile: hamburger with full-screen dark overlay.
- **Footer:** Deep black, Cyan links, Hot Pink on hover.
- **Cards:** Dark glass (rgba white at 3%), 1px border at rgba white 6-8%, border-color transitions to Cyan on hover with subtle glow (box-shadow).
- **Typography treatment:** Oversized Montserrat 900 weight for hero hook (44-46px desktop). Step numbers in Montserrat 900 at 48px with 20% opacity as watermarks. Section labels in 12px uppercase with 3px letter-spacing.
- **Buttons:** All uppercase, 1px letter-spacing. Primary: Hot Pink fill. Secondary: outlined with brand color border. CTA emphasis: Yellow fill with black text.
- **Accent details:** Subtle radial gradients behind key sections (pink for hero, yellow for detection kit). No hard dividers -- sections distinguished by background shade shifts.

### Naming Conventions

All products use the parent brand language:
- "Energy Vampire" or "EVB" appears in every product name
- Action words: Blaster, Detection, Breaking, Challenge, Shield
- Avoid generic self-help product names ("Transform Your Life Masterclass")
- The gamification vocabulary (levels, boss fights, power-ups) should flow into product naming as the brand matures

### Visual Hierarchy

The parent brand (EVB) logo appears on ALL materials. Sub-products get their own title treatment but always within the EVB color palette and typography system. No sub-product should develop its own independent visual identity.
