## Email 4: Day 4 -- Walls & Bridges

**Subject:** Day 4: A 4-minute exercise that will change how you see yourself
**Preview text:** Two walls. Two minutes each. What you discover might surprise you.

---

Day 4. Halfway through.

Today we build on everything you've uncovered. We're going to do something I call the Walls & Bridges exercise. It's simple. It takes 4 minutes. And it will reveal something about yourself you need to see.

**Here's how it works.**

Imagine two walls standing in front of you.

**Wall 1: The Shadow Wall.** This wall is made of your limiting beliefs, your fears, your blame, your excuses. The bricks are the things that hold you back. "I'm not smart enough." "It's too late for me." "My family messed me up." "I'll never be good enough." Write as many bricks as you can in 2 minutes. Don't think too hard. Just write.

**Wall 2: The Light Wall.** This wall is made of your strengths, your blessings, and the people who build you up. The bricks are everything good. "I'm resilient." "I survived things that could have broken me." "My best friend always has my back." "I'm a good listener." "I'm still here, still fighting." Write as many bricks as you can in 2 minutes.

Done? Good.

Now look at both walls. Which one was easier to fill? Which one came faster? Which wall has more bricks?

If the Shadow Wall was easier, that's not a failure. That's a signal. It means the Inner Vampire has been running the show for so long that negativity flows easier than truth. But look at your Light Wall. Those things are real. Your strengths are real. Your blessings are real. The people who love you are real. The Shadow Wall is built on fear. The Light Wall is built on fact.

Most of us have been staring at the wrong wall our entire lives.

Starting today, you build on the Light Wall. You add a brick every single day. You remind yourself of what's true instead of what's loud.

**Today's Exercise:**

Complete the Walls & Bridges exercise. 2 minutes per wall. Then answer this question: which wall have I been living in? Write your answer in one sentence. Post it in the group.

## Email 5: Day 5 -- Set Your Boundaries

Day 5. Time to build your fence.

You've identified your vampires. You've seen their patterns. You've met your Inner Vampire and caught it in the act. You've discovered which wall you've been living in.

Now it's time to do the thing that scares most people more than anything else.

Set boundaries.

I know. I know. The word alone might make your stomach tighten. Because if you're someone who's been drained by Energy Vampires, chances are you've been a people-pleaser your whole life. You've been the one who bends. The one who adjusts. The one who says yes when you mean no, because you're terrified of conflict, rejection, or being called selfish.

Energy Vampires hate boundaries. They'll push against them. They'll guilt-trip you. They'll call you cold, ungrateful, or dramatic. Here's something that took me years to learn: "No" is a complete sentence. You don't owe anyone an explanation for protecting your peace. You don't need to justify your boundaries with a PowerPoint presentation. You just need to say them and mean them.

**3 Boundary Scripts to get you started:**

Script 1: "I care about you, but I can't keep having conversations that leave me feeling drained. I need to step back from this."

Script 2: "I've decided to stop [habit/behavior]. I'm not asking for permission. I'm telling you because I respect our relationship enough to be honest."

Script 3: "When you [specific behavior], it affects me. Going forward, I need [specific change]. If that can't happen, I'll need to create distance."

**Today's Exercise:**
